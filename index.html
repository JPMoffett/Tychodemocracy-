TYCHODEMOCRACY




Democracy Through Random Selection



1. Foundational Principles

1. Random Selection (“Tychos”)

All legislative and administrative officials—at every level—are chosen randomly from the eligible population, similar to jury duty.

2. Third‑Generation Citizenship Requirement

Only citizens who can demonstrate at least three generations of continuous national citizenship are eligible for selection.

◦ This is meant to ensure deep civic roots and commitment.

3. Two‑Year Maximum Terms

◦ All officials serve no more than 2 years.

◦ No reappointment for at least 10 years.

◦ Their normal life expenses (housing, food, healthcare, utilities) are fully covered during service to prevent undue influence or bribery.

4. No Political Parties

◦ All parties, factions, or organized political blocs are banned.

◦ Governance is individual, civic, and duty‑bound—not partisan.

5. No Electoral College / No Districts

◦ The entire country is treated as one unified constituency.

◦ Random selection ensures natural demographic representation without gerrymandering.

6. Direct Digital Executive Election

◦ President + Two Vice Presidents are elected by universal direct vote.

◦ Voting is performed via secure phone/digital system using SSN verification.

◦ The top vote‑getter becomes President.

◦ Second and third become Vice Presidents.


2. Government Structure

A. Executive Branch

President

• 4‑year term.

• Oversees national direction, diplomacy, and crisis response.

Vice Presidents (2)

• Serve as co‑executives.

• Provide redundancy and prevent concentration of authority.

• Serve the same 4‑year term.

Powers Limited by Law

• Cannot create legislation.

• Cannot rule by decree except in narrowly defined emergencies.

• All actions subject to judicial review and transparency requirements.

B. Sortition Assembly (Legislative Branch)

• Approximately 1,000 randomly selected citizens.

• Serve 2-year terms.

• Must pass:

◦ mental health evaluation

◦ non‑partisanship oath

◦ financial interest disclosures

Functions

• Drafts and passes laws.

• Oversees executive actions via audits.

• Controls national budget.

• Makes committee assignments randomly, with experts providing technical advice.

C. Judicial Branch

• Independent, apolitical judiciary.

• Judges chosen via mixed system:

◦ Legal professionals selected by merit-based panels.

◦ A percentage of judicial oversight boards selected randomly.

• Oversees:

◦ constitutionality of laws

◦ executive conduct

◦ anti‑corruption enforcement


3. Anti‑Corruption Architecture

This is the core of Tychodemocracy.

A. Absolute Prohibition of Gifts, Payments, or Favors

Any official who:

• solicits,

• accepts,

• or “is aware of and fails to report”

any form of gift, payment, benefit, promise, favor, employment guarantee, or deferred compensation

during or after time served in office is guilty of high-crimes against the People of the United States.

All involved parties will be immediately Executed for Treason.

B. Independent Anti‑Corruption Directorate (IACD)

• Completely autonomous.

• Staffed by rotating sortition members, investigators, and auditors.

• Has power to:

◦ subpoena communications

◦ audit finances

◦ launch investigations

◦ publicly report corruption in real time

C. Transparency Mandates

All government operations—including:

• votes,

• debates,

• budgets,

• committees,

• executive directives—

are publicly streamed, archived, and searchable.


4. Social & Civic Continuity Measures

A. Life Stability Support

Because officials are pulled suddenly from their normal lives:

• all normal household bills are automatically paid by the state

• employers must hold their jobs (similar to military reserve protections)

• officials receive counseling, training, and civic education

After their 2-year term:

• they return to normal life

• no ongoing privileges

• no pension

• no insider connections


5. Citizen Participation

A. Executive Elections

• Direct vote

• SSN-secured verification

• Single national ballot

• No campaigns permitted

• Candidates may only release:

◦ a biography

◦ a statement of intent

◦ a short policy outline

This prevents money or influence from impacting elections.


6. Stability & Continuity

Because random selection creates turnover:

• terms are staggered

• committees rotate partially, not fully

• expert civil service provides continuity

• long-term planning boards exist but remain non-political and evidence-based


7. Why Tychodemocracy Is Almost Immune to Corruption

1. No parties → no factions to co-opt.

2. Random selection → no career politicians.

3. Short terms → no time to build corrupt networks.

4. Bills covered → no financial pressure on officials.

5. Harsh nonviolent penalties → serious deterrence.

6. Total transparency → secrecy becomes impossible.

7. Independent anti-corruption agency → continuous oversight.

CONSTITUTION OF THE TYCHODEMOCRACY


PREAMBLE

We, the People, in order to establish a just, transparent, corruption‑resistant, and participatory system of governance,

do ordain and establish this Constitution for the Tychodemocracy. We affirm that public service is a civic duty, not a career;

that political parties distort representation; that equal participation is the cornerstone of liberty; and that randomness

(“tychos”) is the surest guarantee against tyranny and corruption.


ARTICLE I — Citizenship & Eligibility

Section 1. Citizenship shall be defined as legal national status transmitted through lineage.

Section 2. Only third‑generation citizens—those with at least three consecutive generations of legal citizenship—shall be eligible for public service positions.

Section 3. Citizenship shall never be conditioned on political, ideological, religious, or economic affiliation.


ARTICLE II — The Legislature (Sortition Assembly)

Section 1. Creation

A national legislative body, hereafter known as the Sortition Assembly, shall be constituted through random selection of eligible citizens.

Section 2. Composition

The Assembly shall consist of a number proportional to national population, not fewer than 500 nor greater than 1,500 members.

Section 3. Term of Service

Members shall serve a term of two (2) years, not eligible for reselection for ten (10) years.

Section 4. Compensation & Support

During service, all members shall have their essential living expenses paid by the national government. No salary, gift, payment, or benefit may be accepted.

Section 5. Powers

The Sortition Assembly shall:

1. Draft and enact laws

2. Approve or modify national budgets

3. Conduct audits and investigations

4. Confirm appointments to expert technical bodies

5. Oversee executive actions

Section 6. Prohibitions

The Assembly shall not:

1. Form political parties or caucuses

2. Campaign or lobby

3. Appoint its own successors


ARTICLE III — The Executive Branch

Section 1. Composition

The Executive shall consist of:

• One (1) President, and

• Two (2) Vice Presidents

Section 2. Election

All eligible citizens may vote directly for executive candidates via secure digital systems

authenticated through Social Security Number or equivalent national identifier.

• Highest vote total: President

• Next two highest: Vice Presidents

Section 3. Terms

Executives serve four (4) year terms, non‑renewable for at least twelve (12) years.

Section 4. Powers

The Executive has authority over:

• National defense

• Diplomacy and treaties

• Crisis management

• Execution of laws

Section 5. Limitations

Executives may not:

1. Create legislation

2. Alter legislative decisions

3. Form political parties

4. Receive gifts, payments, or external benefits


ARTICLE IV — The Judiciary

Section 1. Structure

An independent judiciary shall interpret the Constitution and laws, consisting of:

• A Supreme Court

• Regional courts

• Administrative courts

Section 2. Selection

Judges shall be chosen through a mixed process:

• Career legal professionals vetted by merit panels

• Oversight boards selected via sortition

Section 3. Powers

Judiciary has authority to:

• Conduct judicial review

• Protect civil rights

• Enforce anti‑corruption laws

• Oversee executive legality


ARTICLE V — Anti‑Corruption Provisions

Section 1. Absolute Prohibition

No official may accept any form of gift, payment, promise, or favor during or after service.

Section 2. Penalties

Violations shall result in:

• Immediate removal from office

• Permanent disqualification from public service

• Severe financial and legal penalties, including mandatory imprisonment

• Public disclosure of wrongdoing

• Confiscation of corrupt assets

Section 3. Enforcement

A permanent Independent Anti‑Corruption Directorate (IACD) shall investigate and prosecute violations.


ARTICLE VI — Transparency

All government actions, votes, budgets, debates, and documents shall be public, archived, and freely accessible.


ARTICLE VII — Amendments

Amendments may be proposed by:

1. A 60% majority of the Sortition Assembly, or

2. A petition signed by 5% of citizens

Ratification requires:

• A national direct vote, approved by simple majority.
