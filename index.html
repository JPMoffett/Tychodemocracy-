TYCHODEMOCRACY




Democracy Through Random Selection



1. Foundational Principles

1. Random Selection (“Tychos”)

All legislative and administrative officials—at every level—are chosen randomly from the eligible population, similar to jury duty.

2. Third‑Generation Citizenship Requirement

Only citizens who can demonstrate at least three generations of continuous national citizenship are eligible for selection.

◦ This is meant to ensure deep civic roots and commitment.

3. Two‑Year Maximum Terms

◦ All officials serve no more than 2 years.

◦ No reappointment for at least 10 years.

◦ Their normal life expenses (housing, food, healthcare, utilities) are fully covered during service to prevent undue influence or bribery.

4. No Political Parties

◦ All parties, factions, or organized political blocs are banned.

◦ Governance is individual, civic, and duty‑bound—not partisan.

5. No Electoral College / No Districts

◦ The entire country is treated as one unified constituency.

◦ Random selection ensures natural demographic representation without gerrymandering.

6. Direct Digital Executive Election

◦ President + Two Vice Presidents are elected by universal direct vote.

◦ Voting is performed via secure phone/digital system using SSN verification.

◦ The top vote‑getter becomes President.

◦ Second and third become Vice Presidents.


2. Government Structure

A. Executive Branch

President

• 4‑year term.

• Oversees national direction, diplomacy, and crisis response.

Vice Presidents (2)

• Serve as co‑executives.

• Provide redundancy and prevent concentration of authority.

• Serve the same 4‑year term.

Powers Limited by Law

• Cannot create legislation.

• Cannot rule by decree except in narrowly defined emergencies.

• All actions subject to judicial review and transparency requirements.

B. Sortition Assembly (Legislative Branch)

• Approximately 1,000 randomly selected citizens.

• Serve 2-year terms.

• Must pass:

◦ mental health evaluation

◦ non‑partisanship oath

◦ financial interest disclosures

Functions

• Drafts and passes laws.

• Oversees executive actions via audits.

• Controls national budget.

• Makes committee assignments randomly, with experts providing technical advice.

C. Judicial Branch

• Independent, apolitical judiciary.

• Judges chosen via mixed system:

◦ Legal professionals selected by merit-based panels.

◦ A percentage of judicial oversight boards selected randomly.

• Oversees:

◦ constitutionality of laws

◦ executive conduct

◦ anti‑corruption enforcement


3. Anti‑Corruption Architecture

This is the core of Tychodemocracy.

A. Absolute Prohibition of Gifts, Payments, or Favors

Any official who:

• solicits,

• accepts,

• or “is aware of and fails to report”

any form of gift, payment, benefit, promise, favor, employment guarantee, or deferred compensation

during or after time served in office is guilty of high-crimes against the People of the United States.

All involved parties will be immediately Executed for Treason.
